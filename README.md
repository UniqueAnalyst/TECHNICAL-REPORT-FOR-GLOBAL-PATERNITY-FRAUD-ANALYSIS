## 🎯 Objective

To explore public perception, awareness, and societal beliefs about **paternity fraud** on a global scale. The goal is to identify:
- Knowledge gaps,
- Root causes,
- Affected parties, and
- Suggested solutions.

---

## ❓ Problem Statement

**Paternity fraud**—the deliberate misidentification of a child’s biological father—is a global issue with **emotional, legal, and financial impacts**. This project answers:
- How aware are people of this issue?
- What do they believe causes it?
- Who is most affected?
- Should it be criminalized?
- What role should governments play?

## 📈 In-Analysis Observations

- 🇳🇬 **Nigeria**: Highest reported cases (148)
- 👨 **Males** more aware (91) than 👩 **Females** (78)
- 🎓 **Students** = most aware group (68)
- 👶 **Children** = most affected group (106), followed by supposed fathers (78)
- 🧬 **Infidelity** = most cited cause (89)
- 🏛️ Top solution: **Free/subsidized DNA testing** (85 responses)
- 💰 Most say **parents** should bear DNA test cost

---

## 🔎 In-Analysis Insights

- **Nigeria’s high awareness** is likely due to local survey concentration + socio-legal limitations.
- **Students' high awareness** stems from media and academic exposure.
- **Infidelity** dominates as a perceived cause, showing emotional roots of the issue.
- **The child** is recognized as the biggest victim—underscoring emotional trauma.
- **Gender gaps** exist: Females showed higher unawareness despite higher emotional stakes.
- **Cost-sharing suggestions** highlight concern over public spending but need for access.

## 💡 Observations & Recommendations

### 🔍 Key Observations
- 🇳🇬 Nigeria tops awareness due to demography
- 👦 18–34 = Most engaged group
- 👨 Males more aware than 👩 Females
- 🎓 Students most aware; business roles less aware
- 💔 Infidelity = leading cause
- 👶 Child suffers most from paternity fraud

### 💬 Recommendations
1. **Geotarget awareness** in Nigeria using local media
2. **Focus on youth (18–34)** for engagement and education
3. **Empower women** through gender-targeted campaigns
4. **Leverage students** as awareness ambassadors
5. **Engage professionals** via workshops and seminars
6. **Advocate cost-sharing** policies for DNA testing
7. **Protect children** through mental health & legal support

---

## ⚙️ Optimizations

### 🏛️ Policy Makers
- Fund subsidized DNA testing
- Legal reform for quicker testing resolution

### 🌐 NGOs & Civil Groups
- Redirect campaigns to rural/professional areas
- Integrate education into women-focused programs

### 🎓 Educational Institutions
- Add family law & fraud topics to curricula

### 📺 Media & Creators
- Produce short films, interviews, and shows to raise awareness, particularly in low-awareness regions

---

## 🔚 Conclusion

This project uncovers the **deep social and psychological layers** of paternity fraud. While Nigeria leads in awareness due to local survey focus, the **global trends show strong concern for children’s well-being**, a call for **affordable DNA testing**, and a need for **clearer public policies**. It emphasizes the urgency for awareness, reform, and education across demographics and regions.
